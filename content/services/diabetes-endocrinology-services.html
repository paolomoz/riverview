Endocrine glands produce and release hormones that control key functions, such as growth, metabolism, reproduction and blood pressure. Commonly treated endocrine gland disorders include those of the thyroid, pancreas, pituitary and adrenal glands. When one or more of the endocrine glands malfunction, disruption of the natural flow of bodily hormones can occur, resulting in an endocrine disorder or disease.

Diagnosing an endocrine or diabetic condition can be challenging and often comes with many questions. The experienced physicians at Parkview Physician Group are here to answer your questions and develop a treatment plan that addresses your specific medical condition in an efficient and effective manner.

For individuals living with diabetes, the certified diabetes educators and staff of the Diabetes Education Center at Riverview Health provide education, prevention techniques and information on diverse treatments. Through comprehensive instruction, this patient-focused program helps individuals learn how to better manage their diabetic condition.

Often, individuals living with diabetes enjoy the opportunity to connect and share with others who understand the condition through personal experience. The Noblesville Diabetes Support Group hosts monthly group meetings designed to help educate people living with diabetes about how to achieve the best care and treatments for their individual needs. Meetings are held in the Riverview Health Professional Building on the Riverview Health campus in Noblesville.
