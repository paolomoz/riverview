If you think you're having symptoms of a heart attack, seek emergency medical care immediately by calling 911. Learn the symptoms of a heart attack in men and women.

A myocardial infarction is commonly known as a heart attack. A heart attack occurs when there isn’t enough oxygen being supplied to the heart muscle. If your coronary arteries are blocked, it can limit the flow of blood to heart tissue, causing the heart to beat irregularly or stop beating completely.

Myocardial Infarction Symptoms

During a heart attack, men often have these symptoms:

  • Chest pain or discomfort that ranges from mild to severe
  • Dizziness
  • Nausea
  • Pain that may be felt down the left arm or up into the jaw
  • Shortness of breath
  • Sweating
  • Women may experience the same symptoms as men, along with:
  • Pain in the center of the back or in the shoulder area
  • Unusual fatigue for no obvious reason

Diagnosing Myocardial Infarction at Riverview Health

Cardiologists at Riverview Health in Noblesville, IN, specialize in a wide array of diagnostic tests to evaluate your heart health and potentially life-threatening conditions. Your doctor may recommend performing the following procedures to diagnose a heart attack:

  • EKG (electrocardiogram), a noninvasive test that records the electrical signals of your heart
  • Blood tests, to check for signs of heart damage
  • Coronary angiography (angiogram), an X-ray imaging test where your doctor threads a small tube from an artery in your arm or groin up to your heart to look for blockages in your arteries

Once your doctor has reviewed your test results, he or she will make a diagnosis and create a plan of action for your care.
