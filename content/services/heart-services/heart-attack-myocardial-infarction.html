Treating Myocardial Infarction at Riverview Health in Noblesville, IN

If you think you're having symptoms of a heart attack, seek emergency medical care immediately by calling 911. Learn the symptoms of a heart attack in men and women.A myocardial infarction is commonly known as a heart attack. A heart attack occurs when there isn’t enough oxygen being supplied to the heart muscle. If your coronary arteries are blocked, it can limit the flow of blood to heart tissue, causing the heart to beat irregularly or stop beating completely.

Myocardial Infarction Symptoms

Chest pain or discomfort that ranges from mild to severe

Dizziness

Nausea

Pain that may be felt down the left arm or up into the jaw

Shortness of breath

Sweating

Women may experience the same symptoms as men, along with:

Pain in the center of the back or in the shoulder area

Unusual fatigue for no obvious reason

Diagnosing Myocardial Infarction at Riverview Health

EKG (electrocardiogram), a noninvasive test that records the electrical signals of your heart

Blood tests, to check for signs of heart damage

Coronary angiography (angiogram), an X-ray imaging test where your doctor threads a small tube from an artery in your arm or groin up to your heart to look for blockages in your arteries
