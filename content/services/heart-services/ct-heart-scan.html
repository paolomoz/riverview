A computed tomography (CT) scan, also called a CAT scan, is an advanced type of X-ray test that doctors use to evaluate and diagnose a wide range of medical conditions. A CT scan is a quick, accurate form of diagnostic imaging that allows doctors to get a clear picture of the organs in your chest and abdomen.

Why a CT Scan Is Performed

At Riverview Health, doctors may use cardiac CT scans to:

  • Diagnose the cause of symptoms, including chest or abdominal pain, speech and vision changes, or shortness of breath
  • Guide procedures, such as biopsy or cancer treatment
  • Plan surgery and monitor treatment, such as radiation therapy, chemotherapy, organ transplant or cancer surgery
  • Scan for things like organ damage, fractures or internal bleeding from accidents or trauma

What to Expect from a CT Heart Scan at Riverview Health

A cardiac CT scan will be performed at Riverview Health hospital and may last up to 30 minutes. You’ll lie on a table that moves through a ring-shaped machine, which will take pictures of your chest and abdominal area.

What a CT Scan Can Help Diagnose

A cardiac CT scan can help your doctor diagnose the following:

  • Blood vessel problems
  • Heart and lung conditions
  • Infections
  • Internal bleeding
  • Tumors (abnormal growth of tissue)

Your doctor will discuss the results of your CT scan with you as soon as possible and recommend the appropriate treatment.
