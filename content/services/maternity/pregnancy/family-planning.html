Common Family Planning Questions

Whether you’re growing your family or you’ve decided your family is complete, there are many questions to consider including:

  • When would you like to have your first child?
  • When would you like to stop having children?
  • How many children do you want to have?
  • What if you get pregnant before you plan to?
  • Are you aware of any problems or genetic factors you need to consider?
  • Have you had any previous problems getting pregnant?
  • Are you aware of the various birth control options?

Talk to an Expert at Riverview Health About Infertility Treatments

If you’re ready to have a family and are having trouble getting pregnant, our team of experienced Noblesville obstetricians can refer you to an infertility specialist who can help you pursue the following fertility options:

  • In vitro fertilization (IVF). During IVF, your eggs are removed from your ovaries and combined with sperm from your partner. This process is usually conducted in a laboratory. If your egg is successfully fertilized, it’s then transferred back to your uterus.
  • Donor eggs and embryos. This fertility method uses an egg, which is donated by another woman, and mixes it in with your partner’s sperm. It’s then moved to your uterus.
  • Fertility drugs. Fertility drugs help regulate your reproductive hormones by triggering the release of one or more eggs during your ovulation cycle. They’re most often used for three to six months before trying to conceive.
  • Intrauterine insemination. This fertility treatment involves depositing sperm from your partner directly into your uterus through a catheter from your cervix.

Maternal Fetal Medicine at Riverview Health

For those women managing high-risk pregnancies, our obstetricians can refer you to maternal fetal medicine experts who partner closely with Riverview Health to provide the same great comprehensive care. With a team of experienced maternal fetal medicine specialists, genetic counselors and ultra-sonographers, you can rest assured that you and your developing baby will be in good hands. In addition, strong partnerships with other local hospitals ensure you and your baby have 24/7 access to the top neonatal intensive care units in the area.
