Regardless of your age or athleticism, concussions can be a major health concern. A concussion is a traumatic brain injury (TBI) that’s typically the result of a blow to the head that causes your brain to move around or be jolted. When this occurs, your brain can become bruised and blood vessels and nerves can become damaged.

Schedule an Appointment

If you or your child is involved in sports or other physical activities, we recommend ImPACT assessments at Riverview Health. An ImPACT assessment is a baseline test used prior to receiving a concussion or after you’ve recovered from a previous concussion. It provides a baseline of your cognitive function or a comparison to see how much damage has been done if you do suffer a concussion.

Symptoms of a Concussion

Concentration problems

Difficulty sleeping or a change in sleeping patterns

Dizziness

Double or fuzzy vision

Feeling foggy

Feeling sluggish

Headache

Memory problems

Nausea

Sensitivity to light or noise
