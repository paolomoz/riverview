Heart Failure Symptoms

For most people, the symptoms of heart failure may include the following:

  • Chest pain or congestion
  • Fatigue
  • Shortness of breath with physical exertion that may or may not go away after resting
  • Sleeping better if your head and upper back are propped up on pillows
  • Swelling of your feet or legs
  • Weakness
  • Weight gain

Causes of Heart Failure

Heart failure is caused when the heart is either overworked or has been damaged in some way. When your heart is weakened, the ability to effectively pump blood is lessened, increasing the risk of further complications. Heart failure can be caused by a heart attack and is often the result of untreated heart disease

The most common causes of heart failure include:

  • Coronary artery disease (hardening of your arteries)
  • Heart valve disorders
  • High blood pressure (hypertension)
  • Irregular heartbeat (arrhythmias)
  • Obesity
  • Smoking
  • Substance abuse (like anabolic steroids)
  • Untreated anemia

Heart Failure Treatment & Prevention at Riverview Health

When you meet with a heart specialist at Riverview Health, he or she will recommend a heart-healthy lifestyle—including eating healthy foods, limiting salt intake, getting plenty of exercise and not smoking.

If you have an underlying condition such as high blood pressure or high cholesterol, your doctor will create a personalized treatment plan to fit your health needs. If lifestyle changes aren’t enough, medications or surgery, such as ICD or pacemaker placement, may be helpful in treating your heart failure.
