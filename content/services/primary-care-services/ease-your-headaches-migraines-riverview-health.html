A headache is defined as any type of discomfort or pain around the head, face or neck area. Seven out of 10 Americans have at least one headache per year, and migraine headaches affect about 12 percent of the population. Common Causes of Headaches & Migraines

Migraines and headaches can be caused by:

Alcohol
Birth control pills
Caffeine
Cocoa and other foods
Hormonal fluctuations
Inconsistent sleep schedule
Missing meals
Sinus infection

Do I Have a Migraine or Headache?

Migraines and headaches are similar, but certain symptoms make them different. It’s likely your headache is considered a migraine if your symptoms include:

Absence from normal activities like school or work
Headache that lasts for hours or longer
Moderate to severe headache pain
Nausea and vomiting
Pain worsens when you move around
Sensitivity to light, odors and noise
Throbbing or pounding that’s worse on one side
