A headache is defined as any type of discomfort or pain around the head, face or neck area. Seven out of 10 Americans have at least one headache per year, and migraine headaches affect about 12 percent of the population. Being a woman and having a family history of migraines increase your risk of having migraine headaches.

If your daily activities are hindered by frequent headaches or migraines, find treatment at Riverview Health in Noblesville, IN. Reach out to a primary care provider near you and talk to them about your headache and migraine symptoms to find the best solution for your health needs.

Common Causes of Headaches & Migraines

Migraines and headaches can be caused by:

  • Alcohol
  • Birth control pills
  • Caffeine
  • Cocoa and other foods
  • Hormonal fluctuations
  • Inconsistent sleep schedule
  • Missing meals
  • Sinus infection

Do I Have a Migraine or Headache?

Migraines and headaches are similar, but certain symptoms make them different. It’s likely your headache is considered a migraine if your symptoms include:

  • Absence from normal activities like school or work
  • Headache that lasts for hours or longer
  • Moderate to severe headache pain
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Pain worsens when you move around
  • Sensitivity to light, odors and noise
  • Throbbing or pounding that’s worse on one side
