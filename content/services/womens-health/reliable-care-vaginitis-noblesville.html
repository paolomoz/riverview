Overview

Vaginitis is a type of inflammation that occurs in the vagina, resulting in a vaginal discharge, itching and discomfort. It’s a good idea to see your doctor if you’re experiencing symptoms.

Vaginitis Symptoms

Frequent signs and symptoms of vaginitis may include the following:

  • Change in color, odor or amount of discharge
  • Itching or irritation
  • Pain during intercourse
  • Painful urination
  • Light bleeding or spotting

Types of Vaginitis

The cause of vaginitis will depend on the type you have.

Common types of vaginitis include:

  • Hormone imbalance. Reduced estrogen levels after menopause may result in thinning of the vaginal tissue, which causes itching and vaginal discomfort.
  • HPV. The human papillomavirus (HPV) is transmitted through sexual contact and can cause viral vaginitis, as well as painful warts on the vagina, vulva, groin or rectum.
  • Yeast infections. A yeast infection is caused by an overgrowth of a fungal organism in the vagina.
  • Bacterial vaginitis. Bacterial vaginitis is caused by an overgrowth of bacteria, which can cause an unpleasant itching and vaginal odor.
