Riverview Health offers outpatient infusion and injection therapy for adults in our comfortable infusion center where our skilled, compassionate staff members care for patients seven days a week.

For some patients, the best treatment option is to receive medication through an IV rather than pills. Infusion and injection therapy, or giving medication through an IV, is used to treat cancer, dehydration and infections that cannot be treated with oral antibiotics. Patients requiring infusion therapy come into the hospital periodically to receive treatment and then are free to go home and get back to living their lives.

The Courtney Cox Cole Infusion Center at Riverview Health offers a welcoming environment to patients with natural light and an open floor plan. This new and expanded space is comforting with a soothing color pallet and calming artwork. The Courtney Cox Cole Infusion Center is designed to accommodate a variety of needs with the help of our dedicated physicians and staff.

Patients have the option to choose between private rooms, semi-private suites and infusion pods, or the open social treatment areas—ensuring patients receive care in their preferred environment. This allows for privacy when necessary and social space when appropriate. The large windows offer natural light and a garden view to help time pass and make treatment more pleasant.

Infusion therapy can be a confusing and scary time, so we strive to make the Courtney Cox Cole Infusion Center as welcoming as possible. Additionally, the infusion center staff is happy to put you at ease by answering your questions and providing education and support.

All infusion and injection therapy services require a prescription from a physician with privileges at Riverview Health.

To see a provider at this practice, you'll first need a referral from a primary care provider. (Referral number - 317.776.7444)

Courtney Cox Cole Infusion Center

The Cox, Cole and Peck family members are no strangers when it comes to the Noblesville community and Riverview Health. Chances are you’ve never even thought about an infusion center unless you or someone you know has experienced one. This was the case for Courtney Cole and her family when Courtney was first diagnosed with lung cancer. With the help of a generous gift from the Cox, Cole and Peck families, others going through similar experiences can continue receiving compassionate care close to home—but in a new space that takes into account the physical and emotional needs of patients and their families.

Amenities of the Courtney Cox Cole Infusion Center

  • 15 private rooms with beds
  • 11 semi-private suites with fireplaces and windows
  • Eight infusion pods
  • Comfortable, heated massage chairs to keep patients warm and relaxed
  • Family area and garden
  • TV & USB connection for each person

Conditions that are commonly treated by infusion therapy include:

  • Cancer
  • Infections
  • Gastrointestinal disorders
  • Pulmonology conditions, such as asthma
  • Rheumatology conditions, such as arthritis and psoriasis
