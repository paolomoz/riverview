While most back or neck pain resolves within a few months, many people choose medication, physical therapy and exercises to regain comfort and mobility. However, if your sciatica is persistent and disabling, back or neck surgery at Riverview Health can help relieve pain, as well as improve movement and stability.

Should I Have Back or Neck Surgery?

Disc replacement surgery implants an artificial disc in-between two of your vertebral bones

Herniated disc surgery (discectomy) removes the herniated part of an intervertebral disc

Laminectomy removes part of your bone to enlarge the spinal canal and relieve pressure on nerves in the spine

Spinal fusion surgery permanently connects two vertebrae

Recovering from Back or Neck Surgery

It can take several months to fully recover from back or neck fusion surgery. Through physical therapy and rehabilitation, you’ll learn how to move properly, reposition yourself, turn over in bed, sit, stand and walk in ways that protect your spine, as well as how to avoid twisting your spine while it’s recovering.

Most people have decreased energy for some time, and then gradually return to their normal activities. The orthopedic experts at Riverview Health can discuss the limitations for your individual recovery. Staying physically active and maintaining a healthy weight can help relieve any discomfort following surgery.
