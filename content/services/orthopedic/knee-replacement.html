Rely on Riverview Health in Noblesville for Your Knee Replacement

Also known as knee arthroplasty, knee replacement surgery replaces a damaged knee joint with an artificial joint. Your new joint will let you bend and move your lower leg back and forth without pain.

If you’re suffering from knee pain, ask the orthopedic specialists at Riverview Health if you’re an ideal candidate for a total or partial knee replacement.

Why Orthopedic Surgeons Perform Knee Replacements

Knee pain or stiffness limits your daily activities

Other treatments (such as physical therapy or pain injections) have been unsuccessful

X-rays show severe joint damage that’s accompanied by a loss of cartilage and bone

You have knee pain while resting

Your knee joint is deformed

What You Should Expect

A knee replacement begins with a surgeon making an incision in your knee so the damaged cartilage and bone can be removed before replacing it with plastic or metal surfaces. This surgery can be performed as either a total knee replacement or a partial knee replacement (in which the surgeon removes only one side of the joint).

This is considered to be major surgery that requires anesthesia. You should plan to spend a few days in the hospital, as well as participate in a Riverview Health physical therapy program. You’ll likely feel back to normal within 8-12 weeks, although full recovery can take up to a year.

How Everyday Life May Be Different

Many people are able to return to activities they were not able to do prior to their knee replacement surgery. The vast majority of artificial knee joints still function well 15 years after your procedure, although you can help extend the life of your implant by avoiding contact sports, exercising regularly and maintaining a healthy weight.
