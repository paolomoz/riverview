Comprehensive Care for Shoulder Replacements at Riverview Health in Noblesville, IN

Home Services

Shoulder replacement, or shoulder arthroplasty, is a surgical procedure that’s used to remove a damaged shoulder joint and replace it with an artificial joint. Your new artificial joint will help you move and use your shoulder without pain. When your shoulder pain becomes chronic, ask our orthopedic specialists at Riverview Health about shoulder replacement surgery.

Why It’s Performed

Orthopedic surgeons at Riverview Health use shoulder replacement to address severe shoulder joint damage that’s the result of injury, arthritis or an infection.

You may be an ideal candidate for shoulder replacement surgery if:

  • Less invasive treatments, such as physical therapy, haven’t been effective
  • Pain is present even when you’re not moving your shoulder
  • Severe shoulder pain limits your daily lifestyle
  • You can’t use your shoulder normally

What to Expect with Shoulder Replacement Surgery

Shoulder replacement surgery is performed under general anesthesia, which means you’ll be asleep for the procedure. Our orthopedic surgeons will begin by making an incision in your shoulder to remove any damaged cartilage and bone before replacing it with plastic or metal components.

In most cases, the surgery is a total shoulder replacement, although a partial shoulder replacement may be an option when the damage only involves the humerus head. Following the procedure, you’ll spend a couple days at Riverview Health, and will likely be enrolled in a physical therapy program to help with your recovery, which can take up to a year.

Life After Shoulder Replacement Surgery

Most people find they’re able to return to activities they could no longer do before surgery. While many shoulder replacements still function well 15 years after surgery, you can help keep your artificial joint in good shape by avoiding contact sports and heavy lifting, exercising regularly and using caution when pushing yourself up in bed or from a chair.
