What to Expect During an Ultrasound

During an ultrasound, you’ll need to change into a gown. After lying down on the exam table, your provider will apply a special gel to your skin. Then, a device called a transducer will be pressed against your skin over the area being examined. The transducer sends sound waves into your body and receives the waves that bounce back, creating images on a computer for you and your provider to view. The ultrasound usually lasts about 30 minutes.
