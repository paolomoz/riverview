October 20, 2025

Patient Story

After being diagnosed four separate times, Janet Lome’s decades-long battle with recurrent breast cancer highlights the need for both exceptional medical expertise and deep-seated compassion. At Riverview Health, Janet has found a complete spectrum of care, from early detection to complex surgical and supportive treatment.

Janet’s experience with Dr. Heiser, a skilled general surgeon at Riverview Health, demonstrates how personalized commitment to patient care goes beyond the operating room: “After surgeries, Dr. Heiser would call me just to see how I was. If I had a port put in, or when I had my mastectomy, I knew he would always call. He is a skilled surgeon with phenomenal bedside manner.” This combination of clinical excellence and heartfelt care defines the Riverview Health experience: “Compassionate Care. For Life.”

For Janet, the most profound outcome of her care is simple yet powerful: “I’m alive.” Her experience also underscores the critical role of preventative medicine: “Getting a mammogram saves our lives.”

About Mammography and Breast Imaging at Riverview Health

Early detection stories like Janet's begin with a precise diagnostic image. Riverview Health Radiology & Imaging performs both 3-D and 2-D mammography — covering screening mammography, computer-aided detection and diagnostic mammography — alongside breast ultrasound, MRI, CT and the full spectrum of imaging exams. The department completes more than 55,000 imaging services each year and operates around the clock, seven days a week, with radiologists, imaging technologists and a radiology nurse on the team.

Quality is independently verified: the program has earned American College of Radiology accreditation in Computed Tomography, Magnetic Resonance, Ultrasound and Nuclear Medicine. Through its partnership with the Riverview Health Bone & Breast Center — itself accredited in Breast Ultrasound Biopsy and Stereotactic Breast Biopsy — a suspicious finding on a mammogram can move quickly to follow-up testing and, when needed, to the surgical and oncology teams who cared for Janet.

A routine screening mammogram requires only a physician's order and a short appointment, and it remains the most reliable way to catch breast cancer at its most treatable stage.
