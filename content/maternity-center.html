Maternity Visitor Guidelines >>

With the comfort and safety of you and your baby in mind, Riverview Health provides a family-centered environment to ensure your entire birthing experience is a positive one. Our maternity staff and obstetricians also try to adhere as much as possible to your labor and birthing preferences while ensuring the health of your baby. We have about 600 deliveries each year, including singleton and delivery of twins/multiples. We practice evidence-based care, which means we practice delayed cord-clamping, delayed bathing and immediate skin-to-skin care, which is called "The Golden Hour."

Amenities

Newborn nursery, including Peyton Manning Ascension St. Vincent pediatric newborn hospitalists who are on-site 24/7 and a Level II special-care nursery for infants requiring intensive care

24/7 securely locked unit

Private triage rooms with bathrooms

Eight private and spacious labor, delivery and recovery suites and patient rooms equipped with a comfortable fold-out sofa, cable TV, complimentary Wifi and a private bathroom with bench seating and massaging shower head

C-section surgical suites that are conveniently located in the same area as the other birthing suites

Nurses station patient monitoring

Labor tools such as birthing and peanut balls, birthing bars and mirrors

Certified registered nurse anesthetists (CRNAs) if you choose an epidural

13 private postpartum family suites with cable TV, Wifi, closet space, a private bathroom and a pull-out sofa for your support person

One-to-one nurse: laboring patient ratio

We provide diapers, wipes, baby's bathing items, a swaddle sack and attachments for our hospital-grade Medela breast pump

Personalized time with our International Board Certified Lactation Consultants (IBCLC) to assist with breastfeeding, or formulas and bottles if you plan to bottle-feed

Room service meals provided at your convenience

A nourishment center offering refreshing snacks, drinks and flavored snow cones including sugar-free options for diabetic patients

Spacious family lounges and a nursery viewing room

A Dad's Retreat, donated by the Sheehan Family, that’s equipped with a kitchen area, leather recliners and a large TV

Riverview Women's Boutique with items for new moms such as breast pump accessories, nursing garments, breastfeeding products, newborn items and more

Experts in our Riverview Women's Boutique who will help coordinate insurance coverage of your breast pump

Certified car seat technicians for patients

Level II Special Care Nursery

Babies who need oxygen, IV fluids, continuous observation or CPAP

Babies who are born at or after 34 weeks gestation. These babies may have some health problems, but they’re not so serious that they need a more specialized care.

Babies who are born before 34 weeks or who weigh less than 1,500 grams, until they can be moved to a nursery that provides more specialized care.

Babies who have just gotten out of a NICU and are growing and doing well but are not ready to go home.

Babies who need equipment to help them breathe.
