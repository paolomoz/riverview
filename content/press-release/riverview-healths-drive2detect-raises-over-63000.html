Riverview Health's Drive2Detect raises over $63,000

May 20, 2025

Press Releases

INDIANAPOLIS – Over the weekend, Riverview Health’s first Drive2Detect event raised more than $63,000 for colon cancer detection and post-diagnosis support. In partnership with the CheckIt4Andretti Foundation, the event took place on Saturday at the Motor District Garage Condos in Westfield. 

 

“The support we saw at the first-ever Drive2Detect was incredible,” said Dave Hyatt, CEO of Riverview Health. “The evening reflected that our community shares our commitment to preventing and treating colon cancer. We’re proud to honor John Andretti in such a meaningful way, and we look forward to continuing this mission alongside the CheckIt4Andretti Foundation." 

Drive2Detect is a result of Riverview Health’s partnership with the CheckIt4Andretti Foundation and was centered around raising awareness and funds for colon cancer. The theme for the night was motor sports and included exotic cars, tours of garages, food from local restaurants and live music. There was a raffle and an auction that included luxury items, high-end experiences and motor sports memorabilia. The auction raised over $63,000 for colon cancer screenings for members in the community.  

“This event not only honored my father’s legacy but also made a real impact in the fight against colon cancer,” said Director of CheckIt4Andretti, Jarett Andretti. “Riverview Health and CheckIt4Andretti are committed to continuing to spread awareness and providing resources to aid in the early detection of colon cancer.”  

CheckIt4Andretti focuses on delivering colonoscopies to high-risk, low-income patients. Riverview Health is committed to early detection of colon cancer through its recently announced Endoscopy Center in its Westfield Hospital. 

Drive2Detect is made possible because of support from title sponsors Unlimited Motors, Centier Bank and SIHO Insurance Services and the following sponsors: Exos, AbbVie Inc., Hancock Health, Johnson & Johnson, FUJIFILM and Hirons.  

 

For more information on Riverview Health and CheckIt4Andretti Foundation, visit Riverview.org and CheckIt4Andretti.org.

About Riverview Health 

Riverview Health is an independent community-based health system comprised of a full-service, 156-bed hospital in Noblesville, a 16-bed hospital in Westfield, as well as freestanding combined ER and urgent care facilities. Also included are 14 distinct primary, immediate, and specialty care facilities across Hamilton County. Riverview Health provides comprehensive inpatient and outpatient services in more than 35 healthcare specialties and has been frequently recognized for its clinical and service excellence. Most recently, Riverview Health was certified as a Great Place To Work® and received the Culture of Good Community Impact Award for 2024. For more information, please visit Riverview.org.
